About

Our journey, our dream

  • 2020

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Siyezi Community Development Projects comprised of individuals from Siyezi Village, Silobela, Midlands in Zimbabwe living in the country and abroad who just cannot stand to see their village deteriorate, they are coming together making little donations at free will to rebuild. We would like to partner with individuals, corporations and other organisations to see this dream become a reality. This is one among other areas in Zimbabwe that has been neglected for the longest of times.
    The organisation is currently collecting from their founding City Johannesburg South Africa where it all started though there are other people from the village currently residing in Botswana, Zimbabwe and they have a bank account with First National Bank which they use to collect the donations

  • April 2020

    An Agency is Born

    The first founding committee is elected consisting of 10members and the constitution is drafted.The company currently responsible on the day to day as well as annual financial reports is Global Hope Investments working together with the treasury. SCDP do not have an office as yet they still hold their meetings in public spaces i.e. Parks, for scrutiny the organisations hold records since the very first day for all the collections and jobs done there are images as well as financial statements are available on request.

Mabhalane Bridge

This is one of the two bridges that need proper work.

Stretching for approximately 15km covering our village we do not have proper roads that connects us to local business centres and that makes the delivery of some goods and services limited, as a solution we looking apply for new routes and refurbish the existing roads we have approached the local government about the issue they stated they will be more than willing to provide equipment and machinery as long as the organisation will be able to cover the fuel and labour costs

Water Solutions

This is partly the solution.

Lately we haven’t had good rains so the livestock has been suffering the most so we are looking to drill more boreholes/solar water pumps to curb the shortage of water in the village as you can see this has only been managed at the school and nowhere else.

School Development

Masks and Stationery

We also want to move with the times but at this point our Siyezi Primary School still lack the most basic of things in as far as the technology is concerned. Furthermore the school lacks stationery and not all learners are able to cover those costs which makes life even harder for the kids and the community at large. The aim is to get the school electrified the organisation is looking to fund that project as well and currently they have been able to buy stationery for all the kids at the school as well as assisting with the most basic things such as masks.
